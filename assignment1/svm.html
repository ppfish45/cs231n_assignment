Inline Question 1:¶

It is possible that once in a while a dimension in the gradcheck will not match exactly. What could such a discrepancy be caused by? Is it a reason for concern? What is a simple example in one dimension where a gradient check could fail? Hint: the SVM loss function is not strictly speaking differentiable

Your Answer:

A discrepancy may be caused by a rapid change of gradient in a very small range (compared to the step that the numerical gradient calculator uses) along a certain direction.

In most of the cases we can ignore this discrepancy.

For example, for Hinge loss, if we want to derive the gradient around the zero point, the function is not differentiable, the gradiant check will fail.
